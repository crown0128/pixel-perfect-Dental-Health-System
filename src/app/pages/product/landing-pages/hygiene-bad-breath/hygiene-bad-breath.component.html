What can cause bad breath?

Bad breath, also called halitosis, can be embarrassing for those suffering and family, friends, and colleagues on the receiving end. No one likes bad breath, so it’s no wonder that store shelves are overflowing with gum, mints, and mouthwash - temporary fixes designed to disguise the problem rather than address the cause. Certain foods, health conditions, and habits are among the main causes of bad breath.

Food: Eating certain foods such as garlic, onions, and spices affect your breath. Cleaning your mouth or using products like mints or gum should fix the odour.

Tobacco Products: Smoking itself causes an unpleasant odour. Smokers and oral tobacco users are at a higher risk of gum disease, which is another cause of bad breath.

Medications: Some medications can contribute to dry mouth due to a release of chemicals that get carried out on your breath. Ask your doctor about any side effects you find concerning.

Dry Mouth: Saliva helps cleanse your mouth and remove particles that cause an odour. A dry mouth naturally occurs during sleep, which is where the term “morning breath” comes from. So, it’s best to brush your teeth first thing in the morning.

Infections: Infections in the mouth such as tooth decay, gum disease, and mouth sores all cause bad breath. But also, Infections in the nose, sinuses, or throat are common culprits.

Other Causes: Tonsil stones often form in the back of your throat, they contain odour producing bacteria. They are mostly benign but if they are uncomfortable or consistently cause bad breath, you might want to discuss with your doctor.

Regular check-ups help to prevent diseases in the mouth and might save your tooth from an extraction or root canal in the long run.

Do your gums bleed when you brush and floss? You probably have gingivitis - and it can get a lot worse.

One thing is clear - your body and your mouth are not separate. You may think bad oral hygiene just means bad teeth and gums, but did you know that inflammation (gingivitis) eat away at the gums and bone structure that hold teeth in place?

This is known as periodontitis; a severe gum disease that causes problems in the rest of your body.

What you can do at home to fix bad breath.

Brush and Floss After You Eat: Use a fluoride toothpaste with antibacterial properties, especially after meals. Proper flossing removes plaque and food particles between your teeth, helping to control your bad breath.

Clean Your Tongue: Your tongue is covered in bacteria, so brushing it helps to reduce odour. If you suffer from a dry mouth or smoke, you may benefit from using a tongue scraper to remove bacteria overgrowth.

Avoid a Dry Mouth: Keep your mouth moist by drinking plenty of water. Minimise coffee, soft drinks, alcohol, and tobacco. Try chewing sugarless gum or candy to stimulate saliva. You can also speak with your doctor about medication.

Change Your Diet: Avoid foods that cause bad breath such as onion, garlic, and lots of spices. Where possible, restrain from eating high sugar foods.

New Toothbrush: It’s important to change your toothbrush regularly, either when it starts to get frayed, or at least every three to four months - whichever comes first. Make sure your store your toothbrush in a clean, dry place to minimise bacteria growth.

Regular Dental Check-Ups: You should have a dental check-up every 6 months for examination on oral health, and a professional clean.
