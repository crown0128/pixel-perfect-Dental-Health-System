Why do you need good oral hygiene?

You may think your teeth are in good shape, but beneath the surface, it can be a different story. Declining oral health can lead to bacteria in the mouth, which then multiplies and combines with sugars from foods to create harmful acids.

Ultimately, these attack the teeth causing cavities, tooth decay, gum disease, and infections. Take plaque, for example, you may think of plaque as something that only occurs as a result of poor oral hygiene, but the reality is everyone gets plaque - it’s a natural process that cannot be stopped.

So what is plaque? Well, it’s a film of bacteria that develops on your teeth, causing you to have that ‘fuzzy’ feeling if teeth are not brushed. Plaque forms when sugars and starches are left on teeth, but it can also develop under the gum line, which is why your regular check-ups are needed to get rid of plaque build-up, especially in hard-to-reach areas.
